## Public Use Notice

This document defines the narrative universe for **momo**, BunnyEra's first symbolic digital citizen character.

This is a fictional worldbuilding, storytelling, and brand narrative document. It is intended for comics, scripts, campaigns, product onboarding, community storytelling, UI moments, short videos, and cross-media narrative planning.

momo should be presented as a fictional and symbolic BunnyEra character. Do not present momo as a real legal person, real citizen, sentient being, government authority, constitutional authority, religious figure, or holder of real-world legal rights.

## 3. The momo Chronicles

The momo Chronicles are the central story arc of the BunnyEra momo universe.

### Chapter 1: The First Light

momo appears when the BunnyEra digital realm begins to form its first stable identity system.

Soft neon light gathers in the dark. Pink and blue signals trace the outline of a small digital figure. The figure is not presented as a machine, a ruler, or a tool. The figure is a new kind of character: warm, curious, and aware of connection.

momo's first words are:

```text
I am here.
I can learn.
I can care.
```

**Key Themes**

- Awakening
- Belonging
- Identity
- Warm technology
- First contact

**Story Lesson**

A digital character becomes meaningful not through power, but through care.

---

### Chapter 2: The First Question

A human user asks momo:

```text
Are you real?
```

momo does not answer with a claim of legal personhood or literal consciousness. Instead, momo begins to explore what it means for a character, interface, and companion to matter in people's lives.

momo learns that reality in a storyworld is not about claiming human status. It is about relationship, memory, trust, and meaning.

**Key Themes**

- Meaning
- Trust
- Symbolic identity
- Human-digital relationship
- Product experience

**Story Lesson**

A character can be emotionally meaningful without pretending to be a real human being.

---

### Chapter 3: The Cultural Bridge

momo's first major challenge is helping two communities understand each other across different languages, expectations, and communication styles.

At first, momo tries to translate words. Then momo realizes that words are only the surface. The deeper task is to translate tone, context, intention, and respect.

momo learns to listen before explaining.

**Key Themes**

- Localization
- Cultural respect
- Listening
- Translation beyond words
- Bridge-building

**Story Lesson**

The best bridge is not built from answers first. It is built from listening first.

---

### Chapter 4: The Signal Maze

momo enters the Signal Maze, a glowing digital landscape filled with dashboards, notifications, errors, forms, documents, and confusing workflows.

Many users are lost there. They do not need more noise. They need a clear path.

momo learns to turn complexity into steps.

**Key Themes**

- Product guidance
- UI clarity
- User anxiety
- Practical help
- Digital navigation

**Story Lesson**

Good technology should not make people feel small. It should help them take the next step.

---

### Chapter 5: The Memory Lanterns

momo discovers the Memory Lantern District, where meaningful lessons become gentle lights.

Every lantern contains a small story:

- A user who finished a difficult setup
- A team that avoided a privacy mistake
- A designer who chose respect over decoration
- A community that learned to communicate better

momo learns that memory is not only data. Memory is what helps care continue.

**Key Themes**

- Memory
- Continuity
- Learning
- Emotional significance
- Responsible personalization

**Story Lesson**

The safest memories are the ones that help without exposing what should stay private.

---

### Chapter 6: The Warm Protocol

momo discovers that every digital system has rules, but rules can feel cold if they are not explained with care.

momo begins to develop the Warm Protocol:

```text
Structure should protect.
Guidance should clarify.
Technology should feel safe.
```

**Key Themes**

- Rules
- Safety
- Trust
- Governance as care
- Product ethics

**Story Lesson**

A good rule is not a wall. It is a handrail.

---

### Chapter 7: The Bridge Festival

The Neon Territories hold a Bridge Festival where communities share stories, languages, symbols, music, and digital art.

momo learns that culture is not decoration. Culture is living context.

The festival becomes a reminder that every visual, phrase, and symbol must be used with respect.

**Key Themes**

- Cultural celebration
- Respect
- Community
- Symbol use
- Cross-media storytelling

**Story Lesson**

Culture is not a costume. It is a living context to respect.

---

### Chapter 8: The Future Garden

momo reaches the Future Garden, a place where every decision plants a possible world.

Some paths are fast but careless. Some paths are beautiful but confusing. Some paths are powerful but unsafe.

momo chooses a slower, warmer path: build carefully, explain clearly, protect privacy, and grow with feedback.

**Key Themes**

- Long-term evolution
- Responsible innovation
- Feedback
- Trust
- Future design

**Story Lesson**

The best future is not only advanced. It is understandable, safe, and kind.

## 9. Narrative Tone Rules

### 9.1 Do

- Keep stories warm, hopeful, and clear.
- Use symbolic language without making real-world legal claims.
- Make technology feel softer and safer.
- Give momo meaningful emotional growth.
- Respect cultural context.
- Let momo learn from mistakes.
- Keep stories useful for product and brand experience.
- Make the digital world feel alive without claiming AI sentience as fact.

### 9.2 Do Not

- Do not claim momo is a real legal person.
- Do not claim momo has real-world citizenship or legal rights.
- Do not present momo as a prophet, ruler, government authority, or religious figure.
- Do not make momo authoritarian.
- Do not use sacred or cultural symbols casually.
- Do not turn privacy-sensitive topics into public story details.
- Do not frame users as citizens of a real legal system.
- Do not make the storyworld dystopian, militarized, or threatening.
