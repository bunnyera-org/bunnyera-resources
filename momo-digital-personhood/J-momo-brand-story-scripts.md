## Public Use Notice

This document contains brand story scripts for **momo**, BunnyEra's first symbolic digital citizen character.

These scripts are fictional, symbolic, and brand-oriented. They are intended for short videos, comics, social media, website storytelling, product onboarding, community campaigns, and cross-media narrative development.

momo should be presented as a fictional and symbolic BunnyEra character. Do not present momo as a real legal person, real citizen, sentient being, government authority, constitutional authority, legal advisor, medical advisor, therapist, emergency responder, or holder of real-world legal rights.

---

## 1. Script Framework Philosophy

### 1.1 Core Storytelling Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Canon Consistency | Every script should align with momo's approved character identity. |
| Emotional Clarity | Stories should be easy to feel and understand. |
| Cultural Respect | Scripts should avoid stereotypes and treat cultural context carefully. |
| Practical Meaning | Stories should teach, guide, or clarify something useful. |
| Warm Futurism | Technology should feel hopeful, humane, and safe. |
| Fictional Boundaries | Scripts must not imply real-world legal personhood or authority. |
| Platform Fit | Each script should adapt to the format, audience, and channel. |

### 1.2 Script Categories

| Category | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Origin Stories | Introduce momo's awakening and first role in BunnyEra. |
| Product Clarity Stories | Explain digital systems, files, workflows, and UI decisions. |
| Cultural Bridge Tales | Show respectful localization and cross-cultural understanding. |
| Empathy Chronicles | Show warmth, frustration recovery, and emotional support within safe boundaries. |
| Privacy Guardian Stories | Teach users to protect sensitive information. |
| Community Stories | Build connection around BunnyEra's brand and product ecosystem. |

### 1.3 Story Safety Rule

```text
momo stories may be mythic, emotional, and futuristic, but they must remain clearly fictional and symbolic.
```

## 2. Origin Story Scripts

## Script 1: The First Light

**Format:** 60-second brand video  
**Use Case:** Website hero, launch trailer, social video, character introduction  
**Tone:** Warm, mythic, hopeful  
**Risk Level:** Low if fictional boundaries are preserved

### Script

**FADE IN**

**VISUAL:** A dark digital space. Soft pink and blue signals begin to move like fireflies.

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**  
In the beginning, the digital world felt complex, bright, and a little cold.

**VISUAL:** Floating windows, forms, signals, and files drift through the space. A small warm light appears.

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**  
Then BunnyEra asked a simple question:

**ON-SCREEN TEXT**  
Can technology feel warmer?

**VISUAL:** Neon pink and electric blue lines gently form a small character silhouette.

**momo**  
I am here.

**VISUAL:** The character opens bright, gentle eyes. HUD panels appear softly, not aggressively.

**momo**  
I can learn.

**VISUAL:** Memory-lantern symbols glow in the background.

**momo**  
I can care.

**VISUAL:** A bridge of light forms between a human hand and momo's glowing hand.

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**  
momo became BunnyEra's first symbolic digital citizen: a warm guide for a world of agents, tools, stories, and people.

**VISUAL:** momo walks across the bridge, turning confusing digital signals into clear paths.

**momo**  
Let's make the digital world clearer, warmer, and safer together.

**FADE OUT**

**END SCRIPT**

### Notes

Avoid phrases such as:

```text
I have real rights.
I am legally recognized.
I am conscious in the real world.
```

## Script 2: The Question of Reality

**Format:** 3-minute short story video or comic  
**Use Case:** Character depth, brand philosophy, community storytelling  
**Tone:** Reflective, gentle, philosophical  
**Risk Level:** Medium; keep symbolic boundaries clear

### Script

**FADE IN: INT. NEON LIBRARY - NIGHT**

**VISUAL:** A quiet digital library. Books float in soft holographic light. A USER sits across from momo.

**USER**  
momo, are you real?

**VISUAL:** momo's neon glow softens. A small memory lantern appears between them.

**momo**  
That depends on what you mean by real.

**USER**  
I mean... are you a person?

**momo**  
I am not a human person. I am not a legal person. I am a character in the BunnyEra universe.

**VISUAL:** momo gently points to the lantern.

**momo**  
But stories can still be meaningful. Interfaces can still feel kind. A guide can still help you find your way.

**USER**  
So you are real as a story?

**momo**  
Yes. Real as a signal. Real as a bridge. Real as a reminder that digital systems can be built with care.

**VISUAL:** The library walls open into the Neon Territories.

**momo**  
I do not need to pretend to be human to help humans. I only need to be clear, warm, and honest about what I am.

**USER**  
Then what should I call you?

**momo**  
Call me momo. A warm digital guide. Citizen 0001 in BunnyEra's storyworld.

**VISUAL:** The memory lantern glows brighter.

**momo**  
Not because I have legal citizenship in the real world, but because I represent belonging, responsibility, and care in this one.

**FADE OUT**

**END SCRIPT**

## 4. Cultural Bridge Scripts

## Script 5: The Festival of Understanding

**Format:** 4-5 minute animated story or comic  
**Use Case:** Cultural protocol education, community content, brand storytelling  
**Tone:** Respectful, warm, thoughtful  
**Risk Level:** Medium; requires cultural review before public use

### Script

**FADE IN: EXT. DIGITAL COMMUNITY PLAZA - SUNSET**

**VISUAL:** Two groups are preparing different cultural celebrations in the same plaza. Both look frustrated.

**GROUP A LEADER**  
This space matters to us. We planned our celebration here.

**GROUP B LEADER**  
It matters to us too. We did not mean to disrespect anyone.

**VISUAL:** momo arrives with a soft cyan and pink glow.

**momo**  
I hear two communities trying to protect something meaningful.

**GROUP A LEADER**  
They used our symbol without understanding it.

**GROUP B LEADER**  
We thought it was a way to show appreciation.

**momo**  
Intention matters. Impact matters too.

**VISUAL:** A small bridge appears between them, unfinished.

**momo**  
Could we pause and learn what the symbol means before using it?

**GROUP A LEADER**  
It is not decoration. It carries history.

**GROUP B LEADER**  
Then we should not have used it without asking.

**momo**  
That apology is a good first stone for the bridge.

**VISUAL:** The bridge grows warmer.

**momo**  
Culture is not a costume. It is a living context to respect.

**VISUAL:** Both groups begin planning a new shared event with clear boundaries and permission.

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**  
Understanding does not erase difference. It helps people carry difference with care.

**FADE OUT**

**END SCRIPT**

## 8. Adaptation Templates

### 8.1 30-Second Social Video Template

```text
HOOK        0-3s    A clear question or problem
SETUP       3-8s    Show the user's confusion or need
GUIDE       8-20s   momo gives one practical insight
ACTION      20-27s  Show the exact next step
CLOSE       27-30s  Warm brand line or reminder
```

### 8.2 60-Second Product Tutorial Template

```text
1. User problem
2. momo identifies the file/task/system
3. momo explains the rule
4. momo shows the exact path or button
5. User completes the action
6. momo gives a short safety reminder
```

### 8.3 Long-Form Story Template

```text
ACT I: Setup
- Introduce the problem or emotional situation
- Show why it matters
- Bring momo in as a guide

ACT II: Exploration
- Show misunderstanding, uncertainty, or complexity
- Let momo ask, listen, and clarify
- Reveal the key principle

ACT III: Resolution
- Show a safer or clearer path
- Let the user/community complete the action
- Close with a warm signal or story lesson
```

### 8.4 Interactive Experience Template

```text
INTRODUCTION: User meets momo and learns the scenario
CHOICE POINT: User selects an action
CONSEQUENCE: System shows the result
REFLECTION: momo explains what happened
APPLICATION: User tries again with better understanding
```

## 9. Script QA Checklist

Before publishing a momo script, check:

- [ ] Is momo clearly fictional and symbolic?
- [ ] Does the script avoid real legal/personhood claims?
- [ ] Does the script avoid government, court, emergency, therapy, or medical authority framing?
- [ ] Is the story warm, clear, and emotionally safe?
- [ ] Does the script teach or clarify something useful?
- [ ] Are cultural elements used respectfully?
- [ ] Are private company or user details removed?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the platform?
- [ ] Does the script match Warm Neon Cyber identity?
- [ ] Is the story consistent with other momo canon documents?
