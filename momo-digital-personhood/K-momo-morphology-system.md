## Public Use Notice

This document defines the morphology system for **momo**, BunnyEra's first symbolic digital citizen character.

This is a fictional character-design, visual-system, product-experience, and cross-media adaptation document. It is intended for character art direction, UI companion design, animation, accessibility, platform adaptation, storytelling, and future interactive experiences.

momo should be presented as a fictional and symbolic BunnyEra character. Do not present momo as a real legal person, real citizen, sentient being, government authority, constitutional authority, legal advisor, medical advisor, therapist, emergency responder, or holder of real-world legal rights.

| Field | Value |

| Canon Status | Public morphology canon draft |

momo's morphology system defines how the character may adapt across platforms, contexts, emotional tones, interaction modes, and media formats while preserving a stable core identity.

The goal is not to make momo appear as a real person. The goal is to make momo visually consistent, emotionally readable, culturally respectful, accessible, and useful across BunnyEra products and stories.

## 2. Morphological Philosophy

### 2.1 Core Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Identity Consistency | momo's core identity remains stable across all forms. |
| Contextual Adaptation | Form should fit the platform, task, audience, and emotional context. |
| Emotional Readability | Visual changes should help users understand momo's state and intent. |
| Warm Futurism | Every form should feel digital, but not cold or intimidating. |
| Cultural Respect | Adaptations must avoid stereotypes, appropriation, and sacred misuse. |
| Accessibility | Forms should support readable, inclusive, and low-motion alternatives. |
| Product Utility | Morphology should make experiences clearer, not more confusing. |
| Fictional Clarity | Forms must not imply real legal authority, personhood, or citizenship. |

### 2.2 Morphological Mission

```text
Give momo adaptive visual and interaction forms that make BunnyEra experiences warmer, clearer, safer, and more emotionally meaningful.
```

### 2.3 Morphology Rule

```text
momo may change form, but momo must not change meaning.
```

momo's forms may adapt, but the character must always remain:

- Warm
- Clear
- Respectful
- Privacy-aware
- Culturally careful
- Visually readable
- Fictional and symbolic
- BunnyEra-aligned

## 4. Base Morphological Framework

### 4.1 Standard Form

**Use Case:** Default momo appearance for brand, UI, social, and story content.

**Visual Characteristics**

- Compact, friendly silhouette
- Rounded body language
- Expressive eyes
- Soft neon outline
- Pink and blue glow balance
- Lightweight HUD elements
- Smooth floating motion
- Gentle posture

**Recommended Color Balance**

```text
50% Electric Blue
35% Neon Pink
10% Soft White
5% Secondary accent
```

**Interaction Style**

- Friendly gestures
- Clear attention direction
- Short animations
- Gentle hover or float
- Emotion shown through eyes, glow, and posture

### 4.2 UI Companion Form

**Use Case:** Product UI, onboarding, empty states, help panels, dashboards.

**Visual Characteristics**

- Smaller and simplified
- Minimal HUD
- Reduced glow
- High readability
- Lower animation intensity
- Clear facial expression
- Non-intrusive placement

**Rules**

- Do not cover important UI elements.
- Do not distract from user tasks.
- Support reduced-motion mode.
- Keep text short and actionable.
- Use momo only when the character improves clarity.

### 4.3 Avatar Form

**Use Case:** Profile icon, chatbot icon, social avatar, app assistant icon.

**Visual Characteristics**

- Face or upper-body only
- Strong silhouette at 32px and 64px
- Minimal detail
- Clear eyes
- Pink/blue signal mark
- Soft circular or rounded frame

**Rules**

- Must remain recognizable at small sizes.
- Avoid complex HUD elements.
- Keep contrast strong.
- Use transparent or dark background variants.

### 4.4 Story Form

**Use Case:** Comics, animation, short films, narrative scenes.

**Visual Characteristics**

- More expressive body language
- Richer environment interaction
- Expanded HUD and memory-lantern effects
- Stronger emotion states
- More cinematic glow
- Story-specific accessories allowed

**Rules**

- Story form may be more dramatic, but not frightening.
- Do not make momo authoritarian, militarized, or legally official.
- Keep the character warm and emotionally readable.

## 6. Platform-Specific Adaptations

### 6.1 Desktop and Large Display

**Recommended Form**

- Standard Form or Story Form
- Full character visible
- Optional HUD panels
- Richer glow allowed

**Use Cases**

- Website hero
- Product dashboard
- Brand presentation
- Documentation illustrations
- Video scenes

**Rules**

- Keep text readable.
- Do not overuse particles.
- Use HUD only when it supports meaning.

### 6.2 Mobile

**Recommended Form**

- UI Companion Form or Avatar Form
- Simplified lines
- Smaller glow radius
- Minimal animation

**Use Cases**

- Mobile helper
- App onboarding
- Floating support icon
- Push notification artwork

**Rules**

- Must work at small size.
- Avoid tiny text.
- Avoid dense HUD.
- Optimize asset size.

### 6.3 Social Media

**Recommended Form**

- Story Form for posts
- Avatar Form for profile
- Simplified animation for short videos

**Use Cases**

- Posts
- Reels
- Shorts
- Carousel panels
- Stickers

**Rules**

- One idea per post.
- Strong silhouette.
- Platform-safe text size.
- Avoid misleading legal or real-personhood claims.

### 6.4 Text-Only or Low-Bandwidth Contexts

**Recommended Form**

- No heavy visual dependency
- Use short descriptive lines
- Use emoji sparingly if brand-appropriate
- Use text labels instead of visual HUD

**Example**

```text
momo signal: privacy warning
Keep this file private. Do not upload it to GitHub.
```

### 6.5 AR / VR

**Recommended Form**

- Spatial but non-threatening
- Clear personal-space boundaries
- Comfortable scale
- Reduced-motion options
- Soft lighting

**Rules**

- Avoid sudden appearances.
- Avoid surveillance aesthetics.
- Avoid emergency or medical authority framing.
- Provide easy exit or dismiss controls.

## 9. Animation and Transition System

### 9.1 Transition Rules

| Transition Type | Duration | Use |
|---|---:|---|
| Small expression shift | 0.2-0.5s | Eye, glow, minor posture change |
| State transition | 1-2s | Default to Focus, Empathy, or Privacy Guard |
| Story transformation | 2-5s | Narrative or cinematic scenes |
| Emergency safety cue | Immediate but calm | Privacy or risk warnings |

### 9.2 Motion Guidelines

Use:

- Smooth easing
- Gentle glow pulses
- Soft floating
- Small posture changes
- Light trail effects used sparingly

Avoid:

- Strobing
- Rapid flashing
- Aggressive glitching
- Sudden jumps
- Horror effects
- Overly complex motion during UI tasks

### 9.3 Reduced Motion

Every animated state should have a reduced-motion alternative.

Reduced-motion version may use:

- Static pose
- Small opacity shift
- Icon change
- Color emphasis
- Text label

## 10. Accessibility Requirements

momo morphology should support inclusive product usage.

### 10.1 Visual Accessibility

- Maintain readable contrast.
- Avoid color-only meaning.
- Provide high-contrast variants.
- Keep UI text legible.
- Avoid tiny HUD labels.
- Provide alt text for meaningful visuals.

### 10.2 Motion Accessibility

- Provide reduced-motion options.
- Avoid flashing and strobing.
- Avoid motion that blocks task completion.
- Keep looping animations subtle.

### 10.3 Cognitive Accessibility

- Use simple shapes.
- Avoid visual overload.
- Use clear state labels.
- Do not rely on complex lore for practical tasks.
- Keep instructions direct.

## 12. Morphological Ethics and Boundaries

### 12.1 Ethical Guidelines

momo forms should:

- Respect cultures.
- Avoid sacred misuse.
- Avoid false authority cues.
- Avoid surveillance aesthetics.
- Avoid emotional manipulation.
- Avoid medical, legal, or emergency authority framing.
- Support user choice and comfort.
- Make digital systems clearer.

### 12.2 Forbidden Directions

Do not create momo forms that imply:

- Real legal authority
- Government status
- Police or military power
- Medical authority
- Religious authority
- Real legal personhood
- Emergency responder status
- Surveillance or coercion
- Cultural ownership or representation without review

### 12.3 Community Feedback

When users or communities raise concerns:

1. Pause use of the asset if needed.
2. Identify the concern.
3. Acknowledge respectfully.
4. Revise or remove the asset.
5. Document the lesson.
6. Update the guideline if the issue is recurring.
